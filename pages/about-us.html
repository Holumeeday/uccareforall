Who We Are

A non-profit rooted in community, driven by evidence.

We serve at the intersection of education, empowerment, and infrastructure — and we measure everything we do.

UC Careforall Initiative was founded in Lagos in 2019 with a clear purpose: to educate, empower, and transform underserved communities across Nigeria and Africa. We run structured programmes in awareness, skills acquisition, free healthcare, clean water access, and community development that create change designed to last.

We believe a thriving society rests on safety, equality, and dignity. We do not give aid and walk away. We build capacity, document impact, and share our results with the world — because accountability is one of our core values.

Our Story

Built from conviction, not committee.

UC Careforall was born from a deeply personal conviction. Our founder Ofon Umoh — known globally as The Modeling King — grew up witnessing the impact of poverty, violence, and inequality on African communities firsthand.

In 2019, he made a decision to stop watching from the sidelines. Starting with zero funding and a team of passionate volunteers, UC Careforall began educating communities across Lagos on violence of all sorts, empowering women through skills acquisition, and providing free healthcare to those who could not afford it.

Six years later, UC Careforall has grown into a multi-programme organisation delivering measurable impact across Nigeria — and is now preparing to expand across Africa through the King Walks Africa humanitarian initiative.

One organic post representing Nigerian culture reached over 1 million views with zero advertising spend. That is the platform we bring to every community we serve.

Current Initiative

The King
Walks Africa.

One King. One Country. One Well.

UC Careforall's most ambitious programme to date. Our founder The Modeling King travels across African countries in full traditional Nigerian royal attire — documenting African culture and commissioning one solar-powered water project in each country visited.

The entire journey is documented and shared with a global audience of millions across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube — demonstrating to the world that Africans are solving African problems with African excellence.

Solar water pump systems — minimum 1,000 people served per country
Partnering with local community organisations in each country
Full documentary content distributed across 4 major platforms
Authentic African cultural representation to a global audience

Our Values

Five principles that guide every decision we make and every community we enter.

01
Education
Knowledge is the most powerful tool for community transformation. Every programme we run leaves people more informed than before we arrived.
02
Empowerment
We do not give people fish. We teach them to fish — and then help them build the pond. Dependency is not impact; capacity is.
03
Excellence
We represent African excellence in everything we do — in how we show up, how we document our work, and how we present it to the world.
04
Accountability
We measure and document every impact we create. Our numbers are not estimates — they are verified, field-gathered, and publicly shared.
05
Community
We serve the people around us with humility and purpose. The community is never our audience — they are always our partner.
